CLIMATE CHANGE FROM INCREASING USE OF ELECTRICITY

People in Singapore have been staying at home more due to the rising of COVID-19, resulting in an increase in household energy usage. Aside from the hotter temperature, which boosted the use of fans and air conditioners, more individuals were using electronic devices such as computers while remaining at or working and schooling from home, which increased power consumption even more.

The majority of electricity is generated in thermal power plants, which heat water and produce steam by burning fossil fuels such as coal and natural gas, biofuels, or nuclear fuel. After that, the steam rotates a turbine, which generates energy, which is subsequently fed into the utility system.

We emit greenhouse gas emissions when we burn fossil fuels for electricity, which contribute to climate change.

Energy saving tips
Use LED light bulbs

Changing your lightbulbs with more energy-efficient LED bulbs is a quick and simple approach to minimize your e​lectricity consumption. Modern LED bulbs are up to 75% more efficient than traditional lighting.

Choose energy-efficient appliances

The Energy Rating Label displays the efficiency of specific appliances and equipment. ​For example, a 
5-tick air-conditioner can help you to save about $200 per year as compared to a 2-tick air-conditioner.

Switch off plugs when not in use

Changing your lightbulbs with more energy-efficient LED bulbs is a quick and simple approach to minimize your e​lectricity consumption. Modern LED bulbs are up to 75% more efficient than traditional lighting.

Use fan instead of air-conditioner

When compared to air conditioners, fans use less than 10% of the energy when turned on for the same period of time. Try opening the windows and using a fan.

Stay at 25˚ C

In our humid environment, the air conditioner has practically become a necessity that we keep on all day.  Set the temperature to a minimum of 25˚C for the sake of your wallet and yourself.

Take cold & shorter showers

A shorter shower not only saves water, but also saves on the energy used to heat that water. ​A cold shower completely eliminates the consumption of electricity.
