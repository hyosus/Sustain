CLIMATE CHANGE FROM INCREASING USE OF ELECTRICITY

People in Singapore have been staying at home more due to the rising of COVID-19, resulting in an increase in household energy usage. Aside from the hotter temperature, which boosted the use of fans and air conditioners, more individuals were using electronic devices such as computers while remaining at or working and schooling from home, which increased power consumption even more.

The majority of electricity is generated in thermal power plants, which heat water and produce steam by burning fossil fuels such as coal and natural gas, biofuels, or nuclear fuel. After that, the steam rotates a turbine, which generates energy, which is subsequently fed into the utility system.

We emit greenhouse gas emissions when we burn fossil fuels for electricity, which contribute to climate change.

How Blockchain drives sustainability
Reduce transactional complexity

Blockchain enabled platforms and processes can seamlessly handle numerous transactions more efficiently.

Financial transactions are huge and becoming increasingly more complex,  causeing many delays and backlogs.

decentralised

Blockchain creates a decentralized technical platform based on trust in the distributed network instead of intermediaries. 
This enables facilitating of collaborations between producers and consumers, encouraging people in adopting more sustainable lifestyles, and assisting businesses in improving their sourcing and recycling practices.

transparent & traceable

Blockchain technology also help to foster transparency as it enables the continual update of climate action details from a range of sources, it supports open and transparent data sharing as well.
